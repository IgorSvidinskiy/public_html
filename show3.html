Snowden

Rating: 7.3 / 10 Year: 2016 Director: Oliver Stone

Film description

"Snowden" is a biographical thriller film directed by Oliver Stone, released in 2016. The movie is based on the true story of Edward Snowden, a former National Security Agency (NSA) contractor who leaked classified information to the media about the government's mass surveillance program. Joseph Gordon-Levitt portrays Edward Snowden in the film. The cast also includes Shailene Woodley, Melissa Leo, Zachary Quinto, Tom Wilkinson, Rhys Ifans, and Nicolas Cage. The movie follows Snowden's journey from his early days as a computer expert to his work at the NSA, where he becomes disillusioned with the agency's unethical practices. Snowden decides to expose the government's secrets to the public and goes on the run, seeking asylum in Russia. The film portrays the personal and political consequences of Snowden's actions, as well as the debate over government surveillance and privacy rights. It raises questions about the role of the media, the morality of whistleblowing, and the power of governments to access private information. Overall, "Snowden" is a thought-provoking and intense film that offers a glimpse into the life and actions of one of the most controversial figures in recent history.
